📊 @RohOnChain: Hedge Fund Prediction Market Data Methodology

Roan (backend developer, HFT style) shares how hedge funds use prediction market data to build trading strategies.

🔑 Core Data

  • Becker Dataset: 4M+ trades (since 2020)
  • 36GB compressed, MIT license, free to access
  • Each trade includes timestamp, price, volume, taker side

1. Empirical Kelly & Uncertainty

  • Standard Kelly assumes you know the edge (wrong)
  • Empirical Kelly: Use Monte Carlo to calculate edge distribution
  • Formula: f_empirical = f_kelly × (1 - CV_edge)

2. Calibration Analysis (Longshot Bias)

  • 1 cent contracts actual win rate 0.43% vs 1% implied probability
  • At 80/99 price levels, takers show negative excess returns
  • Longtail bias: -57% mispricing

3. Maker vs Taker

  • Maker (liquidity provider): +0.77% ~ +1.25% excess returns
  • Taker (immediate execution): Systematic losses
  • Advantage is structural, not predictive ability

4. Institutional Methodology

  1. Risk management: Size for distribution, not point estimates
  2. Time-varying strategies: Exploit calibration patterns
  3. Structural positioning: Be maker, not taker
