⚠️ Risk Management

Understanding and managing risks in prediction market arbitrage

📋 Overview

Prediction market trading involves multiple layers of risk. This guide covers the key risk categories and mitigation strategies you should understand before deploying capital.

💡 Key principle: Risk management is not about eliminating risk—it's about understanding it, sizing appropriately, and ensuring no single failure can destroy your position.

🔐 Smart Contract Risks

What to watch

  • Contract bugs: Vulnerabilities in the prediction market protocol itself
  • Oracle manipulation: Price feed or resolution source compromises
  • Upgrade risks: Protocol changes that affect existing positions
  • Bridge risks: If using cross-chain bridges to access markets

Mitigation strategies

  • Use only well-audited, established protocols
  • Monitor protocol governance for upcoming changes
  • Keep exposure per protocol within comfortable limits
  • Consider using hardware wallets for larger positions

💧 Liquidity Risks

What to watch

  • Thin order books: Cannot exit positions without significant slippage
  • Market depth: Large orders move the market against you
  • Time decay: Liquidity dries up as markets approach resolution
  • Event risk: Sudden liquidity withdrawal during volatile events

Mitigation strategies

  • Check order book depth before entering positions
  • Size positions as a percentage of daily volume
  • Have exit plans before entry
  • Avoid concentrating in illiquid long-tail markets

📉 Slippage Risks

What to watch

  • Market impact: Your own orders move the price
  • Spread widening: Bid-ask spreads expand during volatility
  • Latency: Price changes between decision and execution
  • Gas costs: On-chain transaction costs erode thin margins

Mitigation strategies

  • Use limit orders instead of market orders when possible
  • Break large orders into smaller chunks
  • Factor gas costs into expected returns
  • Set maximum slippage tolerance and stick to it

⚖️ Regulatory Risks

What to watch

  • Jurisdiction: Your location may restrict certain activities
  • Platform restrictions: Some markets may be geo-blocked
  • Tax treatment: Varies by jurisdiction and trade type
  • Compliance: KYC/AML requirements on certain platforms

Mitigation strategies

  • Understand your local regulations before trading
  • Keep detailed records for tax purposes
  • Use compliant platforms for your jurisdiction
  • Consult with professionals for significant positions

🛑 Stop-Loss Strategies

Types of stop-losses

  • Hard stop: Automatic exit at predetermined price level
  • Time stop: Exit after certain time period regardless of P&L
  • Thesis stop: Exit when original trade thesis is invalidated
  • Portfolio stop: Overall portfolio drawdown limits

Best practices

  • Define stop-loss levels before entering any position
  • Size positions so stop-losses don't threaten overall capital
  • Use mental stops for strategies where hard stops are impractical
  • Review and adjust stops as market conditions change
⚠️ Important: In fast-moving markets, stop-losses may execute at worse prices than expected (slippage on the stop itself).

📊 Position Sizing Framework

Kelly Criterion (modified)

For those comfortable with mathematical approaches, a fractional Kelly can help optimize position sizes:

  • Full Kelly: f* = (bp - q) / b, where b = odds received, p = win probability, q = loss probability
  • Half-Kelly or quarter-Kelly: More conservative, reduces volatility
  • Never exceed your personal risk tolerance regardless of formula output

Practical guidelines

  • Single position: Typically 1-5% of portfolio for most traders
  • Correlated positions: Reduce size when multiple positions share risk factors
  • Strategy allocation: Cap total exposure per strategy type
  • Drawdown limits: Stop trading if portfolio drops X% from peak

🔍 Operational Risks

What to watch

  • Key management: Lost private keys = lost funds
  • API security: Compromised API keys can drain accounts
  • Infrastructure: Server outages during critical moments
  • Human error: Wrong ticker, wrong size, wrong direction

Mitigation strategies

  • Use hardware wallets and secure key storage
  • Implement API key restrictions (withdrawal disabled, IP whitelisting)
  • Have redundancy for critical infrastructure
  • Use checklists and confirmations for manual trades

📈 Risk Monitoring Checklist

Daily checks

  • Portfolio P&L and drawdown vs. limits
  • Open positions vs. thesis validity
  • Market liquidity conditions
  • News/events that could impact positions

Weekly checks

  • Strategy performance attribution
  • Correlation analysis across positions
  • Risk limit utilization
  • Infrastructure health review

🚨 Emergency Procedures

Define in advance what you'll do in crisis scenarios:

  • Flash crash: Pre-defined response (hold, add, exit?)
  • Platform issues: Alternative access methods, support contacts
  • Personal emergency: Trusted contact with limited access instructions
  • Security breach: Immediate fund movement procedures
