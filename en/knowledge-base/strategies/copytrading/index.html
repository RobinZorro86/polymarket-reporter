📌 Strategy Overview

Copytrading on Polymarket is possible because the market is transparent at the wallet level. The edge comes from finding repeatably strong traders, understanding what kind of trades they are making, and copying only what still survives lag, slippage, and your own risk limits.

💡 Core idea: There may be persistent professional traders on-chain, but profitable copytrading requires filtering. You are not trying to mirror every action. You are trying to capture the tradable part of their process.

🔄 Core Logic

1. Wallet selection

  • Win rate threshold: historical win rate above 55%
  • Sample size: more than 100 trades to separate skill from luck
  • Risk-adjusted return: look for steadier curves, not just explosive PnL
  • Avoid pure leaderboard chasing: visibility often destroys edge quickly

2. Trader-type identification

Directional Trader ✅

Large size, lower frequency, tighter theme concentration.

Market Maker ❌

Often holds both YES and NO, monetizing spread and rebates rather than directional conviction.

High-Frequency Bot ❌

Too fast to copy profitably; your execution lag becomes the entire trade.

3. Execution framework

  • Basket approach: track 5–10 wallets instead of betting on one hero wallet
  • Position sizing: keep single copied trades under 5% of bankroll
  • Slippage guard: set clear max slippage limits before entry
  • Latency discipline: if you cannot execute quickly enough, skip the trade

⚙️ What You Need

  • Data source: Polymarket API plus wallet-level trade indexing
  • Monitoring layer: wallet alerts, dashboard, and market context
  • Filtering layer: conviction score, specialization, and recent performance checks
  • Execution rule: real-time monitoring, but selective entry

⚠️ Risk Warnings

  • Lag risk: you are always slower than the original trader
  • Strategy drift: strong wallets can suddenly change style or stop trading
  • Liquidity risk: your copied size may move the market against you
  • Over-copying risk: not every trade from a strong wallet deserves replication
