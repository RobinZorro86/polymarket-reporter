📌 Strategy Overview

The AI Divergence strategy discovers mispricing opportunities by comparing AI-predicted prices with Polymarket market prices. When AI models calculate fair value that significantly deviates from market prices, arbitrage opportunities exist.

💡 Core Concept: AI models calculate fair value based on historical data, polls, economic indicators, etc. Market prices may deviate in the short term but converge in the long run.
Data-DrivenAI PredictionDivergence Arbitrage

🔄 Core Logic

1. AI Fair Value Calculation

  • Input Data: Polling data, economic indicators, historical voting patterns, demographic data
  • Model: Bayesian hierarchical models + ensemble learning
  • Output: Fair probability of event occurrence (e.g., 62% candidate win probability)

2. Divergence Detection

  • Market Price: Current Polymarket trading price (e.g., 54%)
  • AI Price: Fair value calculated by model (e.g., 62%)
  • Divergence: 8% mispricing
  • Threshold: Trading signal triggered when divergence >5%

3. Execution Strategy

  • AI Price > Market Price → Buy YES (market undervalued)
  • AI Price < Market Price → Buy NO (market overvalued)
  • Divergence converges → Close position for profit

⚙️ Requirements

  • OpenClaw Skills: simmer, ai-divergence
  • Data Sources: FiveThirtyEight API, polling data, economic indicators
  • AI Model: Custom Python model or Simmer built-in model
  • Execution Frequency: Daily scans, execute immediately when opportunities found

📊 Advantages & Risks

Advantages

Data-Driven

Based on objective data rather than emotions

Systematic

Backtestable and optimizable

Low Correlation

Low correlation with other strategies

Risks

  • Model Risk: AI models may miscalculate
  • Convergence Time: Divergence may take time to converge
  • Black Swan: Unexpected events may invalidate the model
