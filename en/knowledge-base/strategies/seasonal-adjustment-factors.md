## 6. Trading Strategy Examples

### Example 1: NYC Winter Cold Snap

**Setup**:
- Market: "Will NYC temperature drop below -10°C in January?"
- Historical probability: 15% (based on data)
- Current odds: 25% (overpriced)

**Analysis**:
```
Historical data shows:
- Jan record low: -18°C (happened once in 10 years)
- Average Jan low: -3°C
- Probability of <-10°C: ~15%

Market pricing at 25% implies:
- Expected value: (0.15 * $75) - (0.85 * $25) = -$10
- Negative EV, don't bet

Wait for odds to drop to <20%
```

**Result**: Market corrected to 18%, entered position at 20%, won when temperature hit -12°C.

---

### Example 2: Miami Hurricane Season

**Setup**:

## 10. Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Ignoring Local Effects

**Example**: Betting on NYC temperature without considering:
- Urban heat island effect (+2-3°C)
- Central Park vs LaGuardia difference
- Time of day variations

**Solution**: Always check measurement location

---

### Mistake 2: Overfitting Historical Data

**Example**: Assuming 2021 Texas freeze will repeat exactly

**Reality**: 
- 2021 was 100-year event
- Probability: ~1% per year
- Don't overweight single events

**Solution**: Use 10+ year averages, not single years

---

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Climate Trends

**Example**: Using 1980-2010 data for 2024 predictions

**Reality**:
- Global warming: +0.2°C per decade
- Extreme events increasing
- Old data less relevant

**Solution**: Weight recent data more heavily (2015-2024)
