Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Polymarket, from account setup to trading strategies.

Account Questions

Which countries/regions does Polymarket support?

Short answer: Polymarket supports most countries worldwide, but US users are restricted.

Supported Regions: Most countries globally

Restricted: United States (CFTC regulations), sanctioned countries

Requirements: Users must be 18+ years old

Account

How do I deposit and withdraw? What payment methods are supported?

Short answer: Deposit USDC via Polygon network. Credit card purchases available.

Deposit Methods:

  • USDC transfer via Polygon (MATIC) network - Low fees (~$0.01-0.10), minutes
  • Credit card purchase through MoonPay - Higher fees, instant

Withdrawal: USDC to Polygon wallet address. Processing time: minutes to hours.

Account

Can I log in from multiple devices?

Short answer: Yes, you can use multiple devices with the same account.

Polymarket allows simultaneous login from multiple devices. Your positions and balance sync across all devices automatically.

Trading Questions

What is the minimum trading amount?

Short answer: No strict minimum, but practical minimum is ~$1 due to gas fees.

While Polymarket doesn't enforce a minimum trade size, consider gas fees on the Polygon network. Very small trades may not be cost-effective.

Trading

How are trading fees calculated?

Short answer: Polymarket charges a 2% fee on net profits when you sell or markets resolve.

Fee Structure:

  • No fees on deposits or withdrawals
  • No fees when buying shares
  • 2% fee on net profits when selling or at resolution
  • Fee is deducted automatically from winnings
Trading

Can I export my trading history?

Short answer: Yes, export available via API or manual download.

Methods:

  • API access for automated exports
  • Manual CSV download from portfolio page
  • Third-party tools like Polybeats offer enhanced analytics

Settlement Questions

What is the basis for market resolution?

Short answer: Markets resolve based on predefined objective criteria and reliable sources.

Each market has specific resolution criteria stated in its description. Common sources include:

  • Official government sources (elections, economic data)
  • Reputable news organizations
  • Sports official results
  • Weather data from NOAA or equivalent
Settlement

What if there's a dispute about market results?

Short answer: Polymarket has a dispute resolution process and UMA oracle fallback.

If market resolution is contested:

  • Community can flag markets for review
  • Polymarket team investigates
  • UMA optimistic oracle used as decentralized fallback
  • Resolution may be delayed pending investigation

Platform Questions

How do I set price alerts?

Short answer: Use third-party tools like Simmer or set up custom alerts via API.

Polymarket doesn't have built-in price alerts. Recommended solutions:

  • Simmer - Automated alerts and strategy execution
  • TradeFox AI - Price movement notifications
  • Custom scripts using Polymarket API
